## Abstract

This whitepaper presents Natural Capital ReBank's (NCRB) approach to tokenizing Phosphorus Credits on blockchain. Phosphorus runoff is a primary driver of harmful algal blooms and lake eutrophication across North America and Europe, creating a $60 billion market for verified phosphorus reduction credits growing at 16% CAGR. NCRB enables transparent, on-chain trading of phosphorus credits with full watershed delivery modeling and state-program compliance documentation.

## 3. Quality Bands & Pricing

Phosphorus credit quality is determined by the **delivery ratio** — a measure of how much of the reduced phosphorus load actually reaches the regulated water body based on watershed position.

| Quality Band | Delivery Ratio | Source Type | Price Range | NCRB Tokenization |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **High Delivery** | 0.8–1.0 (near bay) | Municipal WWT, point source | $30–$75/kg P | ✅ Eligible |
| **Medium Delivery** | 0.5–0.8 (mid-watershed) | Watershed management, BMPs | $15–$40/kg P | ✅ Eligible |
| **Standard Delivery** | 0.3–0.5 (upstream) | Agricultural conservation | $8–$25/kg P | ✅ Eligible |
| **Low Delivery** | &lt;0.3 (far upstream) | Upland practices | $5–$15/kg P | ⚠️ Trading Ratio Applied |

## 4. Token Issuance Criteria

To qualify for NCRB tokenization, phosphorus credits must meet all of the following criteria:

- **State certification** (Ohio EPA, Vermont DEC, Maryland MDE)
- **Third-party verification** mandatory
- **Delivery ratio** based on watershed modeling
- **2:1 trading ratio** for uncertainty (non-point to point source)
- **Compliance with** Lake Champlain, Chesapeake Bay, or Great Lakes TMDL programs

## 5. Quality & Impact Framework

NCRB employs a programmatic quality scoring system (0–100) across six weighted dimensions:

| Dimension | Weight | Key Inputs |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Technical Quality | 25% | Delivery ratio, source type, watershed modeling accuracy |
| Additionality | 20% | BMP additionality vs baseline practices |
| Permanence | 20% | Duration of BMP implementation, maintenance commitments |
| Certification Level | 15% | State certification tier, TMDL program compliance |
| Social Impact | 12% | Agricultural community support, lake water quality for communities |
| Vintage/Condition | 8% | Compliance year, monitoring data freshness |
