## 3. Quality Bands & Pricing

Agricultural land quality follows the USDA Land Capability Classification system, which evaluates soil suitability for sustained production:

| Land Capability Class | Quality Grade | Description | Price Range (US Avg) | NCRB Tokenization |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **Class I** | Prime Farmland | Minimal limitations, highest productivity | $8,000–$20,000/acre | ✅ Eligible |
| **Class II** | Prime Farmland | Moderate limitations, good management | $5,000–$12,000/acre | ✅ Eligible |
| **Class III** | Farmland of Statewide Importance | Severe limitations, special practices | $3,000–$8,000/acre | ✅ Eligible |
| **Class IV** | Marginal Farmland | Very severe limitations, careful management | $1,500–$5,000/acre | ⚠️ Review Required |
| **Class V–VIII** | Non-Cropland | Pasture, forest, recreation only | $500–$3,000/acre | ❌ Not Eligible |

### 3.1 Subclass Limitations

- **e** — Erosion risk
- **w** — Water/drainage issues
- **s** — Soil depth limitations
- **c** — Climate constraints

## 4. Token Issuance Criteria

To qualify for NCRB tokenization, agricultural land must meet all of the following criteria:

- **USDA NRCS Land Capability Classification** (Classes I–IV only)
- **Clear title and ownership verification**
- **LESA (Land Evaluation and Site Assessment)** score documentation
- **Organic certification** adds 20–50% premium (USDA Organic certified)
- **Conservation easements** documented separately

## 5. Quality & Impact Framework

NCRB employs a programmatic quality scoring system (0–100) across six weighted dimensions:

| Dimension | Weight | Key Inputs |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Technical Quality | 25% | USDA land capability class, soil survey data, LESA score |
| Additionality | 20% | Conservation commitments, sustainable practice adoption |
| Permanence | 20% | Title clarity, easement terms, long-term use restrictions |
| Certification Level | 15% | USDA certification, organic status, FSA registration |
| Social Impact | 12% | Local farmer employment, food security, rural community impact |
| Vintage/Condition | 8% | Survey recency, soil health monitoring data freshness |
