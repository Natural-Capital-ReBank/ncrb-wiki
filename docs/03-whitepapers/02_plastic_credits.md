## Abstract

This whitepaper presents Natural Capital ReBank's (NCRB) approach to tokenizing Plastic Credits on blockchain. The plastic credit market is one of the fastest-growing environmental credit markets, with $150 billion in market size and 25% CAGR — driven by growing corporate plastic-neutral commitments and Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) regulations worldwide. NCRB provides institutional-grade infrastructure to bring transparency, liquidity, and verifiability to this high-growth market.

## 4. Token Issuance Criteria

To qualify for NCRB tokenization, plastic credits must meet all of the following criteria:

- Minimum **Plastic Neutral certification** from Tier 1/2 registries
- **Third-party verification** (rePurpose Global, Plastic Bank, Verra)
- **Geographic tracking** (collection location, processing facility)
- **Social impact documentation** for ocean-bound plastic

---

## 5. Quality & Impact Framework

NCRB employs a programmatic quality scoring system (0–100) across six weighted dimensions:

| Dimension | Weight | Key Inputs |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Technical Quality | 25% | Certification tier, waste stream verification, processing documentation |
| Additionality | 20% | Barrier analysis, market access barriers for collection workers |
| Permanence | 20% | End-of-life processing guarantees, material traceability |
| Certification Level | 15% | Registry tier, audit frequency, chain-of-custody verification |
| Social Impact | 12% | Waste picker livelihoods, community health, gender inclusion |
| Vintage/Condition | 8% | Collection year, processing data freshness |

### 5.1 Credit Rating Bands

## 6. SDG/ESG Impact Tracking

NCRB tracks the following impact metrics for plastic credits:

- **Plastic Waste Removed:** kg per year by material type
- **Geographic Coverage:** Collection region and ecosystem type
- **Social Co-Benefits:** Waste picker income, jobs created, health improvements
- **Processing Traceability:** End-of-life material pathway

**CSRD/TNFD Mapping:** Plastic metrics align with CSRD E2 (Water/Pollution) and TNFD Nature Impact and Dependency pillars.
